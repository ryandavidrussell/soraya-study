# Advisory Board Role

**Status:** in design  
**Authority:** Article III (Structural Humility) and Article VI (Institutions) of the Kaleidoworks Constitution

---

## Purpose

The advisory board holds the external interpretation authority that neither the runtime nor the operator may hold. It exists to make the governance structure non-decorative.

## Constitutional grounding

Article IV generates two governance commitments:

1. No party that a measurement is about may hold a verdict immune to challenge.
2. Interpretation and decision authority must sit with a party that has no stake in the outcome.

The operator is a stakeholder — in the framework working. Therefore the operator may not be the final interpretive authority on what the measurements mean. The advisory board fills that role.

## Structural requirements

To satisfy the constitutional constraint, advisory board members must:

- Have no financial stake in Kaleidoworks or Soraya.
- Have no reputational stake in the specific governance framework being correct.
- Have standing to publish independent assessments of the framework's claims.
- Have access to the full claim registry, instrumentation specs, and study data.
- Have authority to flag a claim as insufficiently supported — a flag the operator cannot override without publishing a response.

## What the advisory board is NOT

- Not an endorsement body that validates the framework by association.
- Not a body that reviews outputs and approves claims made by the operator.
- Not a body whose authority can be dismissed because the operator disagrees with a finding.

## Current status

Advisory board not yet constituted. This document defines the structural role the board must fill before any claim that requires external interpretation authority can be considered validated.

## Relation to external validation protocol

The advisory board and the external validation protocol are two parts of the same structure. The protocol defines what requires external validation. The advisory board provides the party with standing to perform it.
